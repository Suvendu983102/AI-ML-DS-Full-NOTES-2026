South-East
South-West
North-East
North-West
Solution:

Let's trace the path:

1. Start at origin, go 10m South → (0, -10)

2. Turn left (East), walk 25m → (25, -10)

3. Turn right (South), walk 5m → (25, -15)

4. Turn left (East), walk 10m → (35, -15)

Final position is 35m East and 15m South from start → South-East

Clock Formulas

Minute Hand: 360° in 60 minutes = 6° per minute

Hour Hand: 360° in 12 hours = 30° per hour = 0.5° per minute

Relative Speed: 6° - 0.5° = 5.5° per minute

Angle between hands: |30H - 5.5M|

Time to coincide: 65⁵⁄₁₁ minutes (every 65.4545 minutes)

Clock Problems Practice

Problem: At what time between 4 and 5 will the hands of a clock be at right angles?
4:05:27
4:05:27 & 4:38:11
4:38:11 only
4:21:49
Solution:

Using formula: Time = (5H ± 15) × ¹²⁄₁₁ minutes

When minute hand is 15 minutes ahead:

Time = (5×4 + 15) × ¹²⁄₁₁ = (20+15)×¹²⁄₁₁ = 35×¹²⁄₁₁ = 420/11 = 38.18 minutes

When minute hand is 15 minutes behind:

Time = (5×4 - 15) × ¹²⁄₁₁ = (20-15)×¹²⁄₁₁ = 5×¹²⁄₁₁ = 60/11 = 5.45 minutes

Convert to minutes:seconds:

38.18 minutes = 38 minutes 11 seconds → 4:38:11

5.45 minutes = 5 minutes 27 seconds → 4:05:27

Calendar Problems Practice

Problem 1: If today is Monday, what day will it be after 100 days?
Monday
Tuesday
Wednesday
Thursday
Solution:

100 days = 14 weeks + 2 days

14 weeks = Monday (back to same day)

Monday + 2 days = Wednesday

Therefore, after 100 days it will be Wednesday.

Problem 2: What was the day on 15th August, 1947?
Thursday
Saturday
Friday
Sunday
Solution:

Calculate odd days from 1900 to 1947:

1900-2000: 0 odd days (century not divisible by 400)

1901-1947: 47 years = 11 leap years + 36 ordinary years

Odd days = (11×2 + 36×1) = 58 days = 8 weeks + 2 odd days

Days in 1947 till Aug 15: Jan(31)+Feb(28)+Mar(31)+Apr(30)+May(31)+Jun(30)+Jul(31)+Aug(15) = 227 days

227 days = 32 weeks + 3 odd days

Total odd days = 2 + 3 = 5

Day code 5 = Friday

Time & Distance Practice

Problem 1: A train covers a distance of 360 km in 5 hours. What is its speed in m/s?
20 m/s
25 m/s
18 m/s
22 m/s
Solution:

Speed in km/h = 360 ÷ 5 = 72 km/h

Convert to m/s: 72 × ⁵⁄₁₈ = 4 × 5 = 20 m/s

Therefore, the speed is 20 m/s.

Problem 2: Two trains start at the same time from stations 300 km apart and travel towards each other at 40 km/h and 60 km/h respectively. When will they meet?
2 hours
2.5 hours
3 hours
3.5 hours
Solution:

Relative speed = 40 + 60 = 100 km/h

Distance = 300 km

Time = Distance ÷ Relative Speed = 300 ÷ 100 = 3 hours

Therefore, they will meet after 3 hours.

Problem 3: A person travels from A to B at 30 km/h and returns at 40 km/h. What is the average speed for the entire journey?
35 km/h
34.29 km/h
32 km/h
33.33 km/h
Solution:

Let distance = d km

Time from A to B = d/30 hours

Time from B to A = d/40 hours

Total time = d/30 + d/40 = (4d + 3d)/120 = 7d/120 hours

Total distance = 2d km

Average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time = 2d ÷ (7d/120) = 2d × 120/7d = 240/7 ≈ 34.29 km/h

Time & Work Formulas

Work = Efficiency × Time

Efficiency = Work ÷ Time

Time = Work ÷ Efficiency

Combined work: 1/(1/a + 1/b) days

Work done = (Efficiency) × (Time worked)

Remaining work = 1 - (Work done)

Time & Work Practice

Problem 1: A can complete a work in 12 days, and B can complete it in 18 days. In how many days will they complete the work together?
6 days
8 days
7.2 days
9 days
Solution:

A's 1 day work = 1/12

B's 1 day work = 1/18

Combined 1 day work = 1/12 + 1/18 = (3 + 2)/36 = 5/36

Time taken together = 1 ÷ (5/36) = 36/5 = 7.2 days

Problem 2: A and B together can do a piece of work in 6 days. A alone can do it in 10 days. In how many days can B alone do it?
12 days
14 days
13 days
15 days
Solution:

(A+B)'s 1 day work = 1/6

A's 1 day work = 1/10

B's 1 day work = 1/6 - 1/10 = (5 - 3)/30 = 2/30 = 1/15

B alone can complete in 15 days

Problem 3: 10 men can complete a work in 15 days. How many men are needed to complete the work in 6 days?
20 men
22 men
25 men
24 men
Solution:

Total work = 10 men × 15 days = 150 man-days

To complete in 6 days: 150 man-days ÷ 6 days = 25 men

Therefore, 25 men are needed.
